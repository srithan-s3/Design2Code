Comprehensive Color Vision Support

Our extension addresses all major types of color vision deficiencies

P

Protanopia

Type: Red-Green
Prevalence: 1% of males
Effect: Missing L-cones, red appears black, difficulty with red-green differentiation
D

Deuteranopia

Type: Red-Green
Prevalence: 1% of males
Effect: Missing M-cones, greens appear beige, reds appear brownish-yellow
T

Tritanopia

Type: Blue-Yellow
Prevalence: 0.003% of population
Effect: Missing S-cones, blue appears green, yellow appears violet
M

Monochromacy

Type: Complete Color Blindness
Prevalence: 0.0001% of population
Effect: Sees only in black, white, and shades of gray
Pm

Protanomaly

Type: Red-Weak
Prevalence: 1% of males
Effect: L-cones detect too much green light
Dm

Deuteranomaly

Type: Green-Weak
Prevalence: 5% of males
Effect: M-cones detect too much red light
Tm

Tritanomaly

Type: Blue-Weak
Prevalence: 0.01% of population
Effect: Reduced blue sensitivity, blue appears greenish
A

Achromatopsia

Type: Complete Color Blindness
Prevalence: 0.003% of population
Effect: No color vision, light sensitivity, poor vision
